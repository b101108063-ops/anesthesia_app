# Chapter 11: Haematology 血液學

## Anaemia 貧血

Anaemia is defined as Hb below normal for age and sex (<130 g/L adult males, <120 g/L adult females).

### Common Causes

- Blood loss (acute or chronic)
- Bone marrow failure (infiltration by tumour or suppression by drugs)
- Megaloblastic anaemias (folate or vitamin B₁₂ deficiency)
- Complex anaemias (renal failure, RA, hypothyroidism)
- Haemolytic anaemias (inherited or acquired)
- Chronic disease/inflammation

### Clinical Features

- Associated with fatigue, dyspnoea, palpitations, headaches and angina
- Severity often reflects speed of onset more than degree of anaemia

### Perioperative Blood Transfusion

| Patient Condition | Transfusion Trigger |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Red cell transfusion | Hb <70 g/L |
| Patients with IHD - mild | Accept Hb 70–80 g/L |
| Patients with IHD - moderate | Accept Hb 80–90 g/L |
| Patients with IHD - severe (recent MI, unstable angina) | Accept Hb 100 g/L or higher |

## Sickle Cell Disease (SCD) 鐮刀型紅血球疾病

Sickle cell disease is caused by inheriting sickling haemoglobinopathies. The pathology is primarily due to vaso-occlusion by sickled red cells, leading to haemolysis and tissue infarction.

### Precipitating Factors

- Hypoxia
- Hypothermia
- Pyrexia
- Acidosis
- Dehydration
- Infection

### Types

- **HbSS (homozygous):** Sickle cell anaemia
- **HbAS (heterozygous):** Sickle cell trait - sickling uncommon (<50% HbS)
- **HbSC disease:** Compound heterozygosity for HbS and HbC

### Clinical Features

- Vaso-occlusive crises (commonest cause of morbidity)
- Acute chest syndrome
- Aplastic crises
- Sequestration crises
- Proliferative retinopathy

### Preoperative Preparation

- Always seek expert advice from haematologist well before surgery
- Send sample for group and antibody screening well in advance
- All patients from high prevalence areas should have sickle test preoperatively

### Perioperative Management

- Dehydration: allow oral fluids as late as possible; pre- and postoperative IV fluids
- Hypoxia: pulse oximetry and prophylactic O₂
- Prophylactic antibiotic cover
- Avoid hypothermia (use fluid warmer, Bair Hugger®)
- Regional anaesthesia is not contraindicated
- Tourniquets can be used if limbs are meticulously exsanguinated

## Porphyria 紫質症

The porphyrias are a group of diseases with an enzyme defect in haem synthesis, leading to accumulation of porphyrin precursors.

### Types of Acute Porphyria

- **Acute intermittent porphyria** (commonest)
- **Variegate porphyria**
- **Hereditary coproporphyria**

### Precipitating Factors

- Drugs (increase P450 enzyme demand)
- Stress
- Infection
- Alcohol
- Menstruation
- Pregnancy
- Starvation and dehydration

### Symptoms

- Acute abdominal pain
- Vomiting
- Motor and sensory peripheral neuropathy
- Autonomic dysfunction
- Cranial nerve palsies
- Mental disturbances, coma, convulsions

### Anaesthetic Management

Many drugs can trigger porphyric crises. Use the website www.drugs-porphyria.org for reference.

| Desflurane | Desflurane | Halothane |

**Key principles:**

- Minimise stress - consider premedication with midazolam
- Minimise preoperative fasting - use 5% glucose/0.9% sodium chloride IV
- Regional anaesthesia useful, but avoid in acute porphyric crises
- PONV prophylaxis especially important

## Rare Blood Disorders

### Hereditary Spherocytosis

- Autosomal dominant condition with smaller surface to volume ratio erythrocytes
- Splenomegaly common
- Splenectomy leads to 50–70% increase in red cell survival
- No particular anaesthetic considerations

### Glucose-6-Phosphate Dehydrogenase (G6PD) Deficiency

- X-linked trait
- Affords protection against malaria
- Drugs producing methaemoglobinaemia are contraindicated (nitroprusside, prilocaine)
- Ingestion of broad beans causes haemolysis (favism)
- Avoid drugs causing oxidative stress

## Disorders of Coagulation

### Haemophilia A and B

**Haemophilia A:** Factor VIII deficiency (X-linked recessive)
**Haemophilia B:** Factor IX deficiency (Christmas disease)

**Severity:**

| Severity | Factor Level |
|----------|-------------|
| Severe | <1 IU/dL (<1%) |
| Moderate | 1–5 IU/dL (1–5%) |
| Mild | 5–40 IU/dL (5–40%) |

**Perioperative Management:**

- Replace deficient factor preoperatively to achieve:
  - Minor surgery: 50–80% normal
  - Major surgery: 80–100% normal (maintain for 1–2 weeks)
- Consult haematologist
- Usetranexamic acid as adjunct
- Avoid IM injections
- Regional techniques only after factor replacement and in consultation with haematologist

## Thrombocytopenia 血小板減少症

### Causes

- Decreased platelet production
- Increased platelet destruction
- Splenic sequestration

### Platelet Count and Bleeding Risk

| Platelet Count | Bleeding Risk |
|---------------|--------------|
| >100 × 10⁹/L | Minimal - safe for neuraxial |
| 50–100 × 10⁹/L | Low - assess risk/benefit |
| 20–50 × 10⁹/L | Moderate - avoid regional if possible |
| <20 × 10⁹/L | High - major bleeding risk |

### Drug-Induced

- Heparin-induced thrombocytopenia (HIT)
- GPIIb/IIIa inhibitors

## Anticoagulants 抗凝血劑

| Table: Perioperative Management of Anticoagulants |
|---|
| **Drug** | **Preoperative** | **Reversal** |
| Warfarin | Stop 5 days before | Vitamin K, FFP, PCC |
| DOACs (apixaban, rivaroxaban) | Stop 48h (low bleeding risk) | Andexanet alfa |
| Dabigatran | Stop 48-96h (renal function) | Idarucizumab |
| LMWH (prophylactic) | Stop 12h | Protamine (partial) |
| LMWH (therapeutic) | Stop 24h | Protamine (partial) |

### Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs)

| DOAC | Half-life | Renal Clearance | Stop Before Surgery |
|------|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Apixaban | 12h | 27% | 48h |
| Rivaroxaban | 5-9h | 33% | 48h |
| Dabigatran | 12-17h | 80% | 48-96h (renal) |

## Antiplatelet Drugs 抗血小板藥物

| Drug | Mechanism | Surgery Stop | Reversal |
|------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Aspirin | COX inhibitor | 7-10 days (if possible) | DDAVP |
| Clopidogrel | P2Y12 inhibitor | 5-7 days | Platelet transfusion |
| Prasugrel | P2Y12 inhibitor | 7 days | Platelet transfusion |
| Ticagrelor | P2Y12 inhibitor | 5 days | Platelet transfusion |

**Dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT):** Usually for stents - balance stent thrombosis risk vs surgical bleeding

## Fibrinolytic Drugs 血栓溶解藥物

- **Examples:** Alteplase, tenecteplase, streptokinase
- **Stop:** Usually within 3-5 days depending on agent
- **Surgery timing:** Usually delayed 3-5 days
- **Reversal:** Antifibrinolytics (tranexamic acid)

| Aprotinin | Reduce bleeding | Loading + infusion |

| Recombinant Factor VIIa | Haemophilia with inhibitors | Per haematologist |

## Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation (DIC) 彌散性血管內凝血

**Causes:**

- Sepsis
- Trauma
- Malignancy
- Obstetric complications
- Severe transfusion reactions

**Diagnosis:**

- Prolonged PT/PTT
- Low fibrinogen
- Low platelets
- Elevated D-dimer
- Schistocytes on blood film

**Management:**

- Treat underlying cause
- Supportive care
- Blood product replacement (FFP, platelets, cryoprecipitate)
- Consider heparin in chronic DIC

## Hypercoagulability Syndromes 血栓體質

### Inherited

- Factor V Leiden (activated protein C resistance)
- Prothrombin gene mutation
- Protein C/S deficiency
- Antithrombin deficiency

### Acquired

- Antiphospholipid syndrome
- Heparin-induced thrombocytopenia (HIT)
- Malignancy
- Nephrotic syndrome

### Antiphospholipid Syndrome

- Associated with thrombosis and pregnancy loss
- Lupus anticoagulant
- Anticardiolipin antibodies
- Anaesthetic implications:
  - May require anticoagulation bridging
  - Avoid oesophageal Doppler (thrombosis risk)
  - Consider thrombosis prophylaxis
