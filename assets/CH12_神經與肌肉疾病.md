## Parkinson's Disease 巴金森氏症

Parkinson's disease (PD) is a multisystem neurological disorder characterised by a triad of muscle rigidity, bradykinesia and a resting tremor. Loss of dopaminergic neurones in the substantia nigra results in dopamine deficiency.

### Drug Therapies

| Table 12.1: Drug Therapies in Parkinson's Disease |
|---|
| **Class** | **Examples** | **Notes** |
| Dopamine precursors | Levodopa + carbidopa | Short half-life; frequent administration |
| DOPA decarboxylase inhibitor | Levodopa + benserazide | - |
| Dopamine agonists (oral) | Pramipexole, Ropinirole | Adjunctive therapy |
| Dopamine agonists (transdermal) | Rotigotine patch | - |
| Dopamine agonists (SC) | Apomorphine | Highly emetogenic |
| MAO-B inhibitors | Selegiline, Rasagiline | Avoid pethidine and TCA |
| COMT inhibitors | Entacapone, Tolcapone | Caution with drugs metabolised by COMT |
| Anticholinergics | Procyclidine, Benztropine | Limited efficacy; confusion, urinary retention |

### Preoperative Assessment

- **Airway:** Dysphagia or sialorrhoea suggests increased risk of airway compromise
- **Respiratory:** Rigidity and bradykinesia may impair respiratory function
- **Cardiovascular:** Arrhythmias or orthostatic hypotension
- **GI:** Dysphagia, gastroparesis, GORD
- **Delirium:** Common (up to 60%)

### Drugs to Avoid

- **Antiemetics:** Prochlorperazine, droperidol, metoclopramide (dopamine antagonists)
- **Pethidine** with selegiline (serotonin syndrome risk)
- **Synthetic opioids** (dose-related muscle rigidity)
- **Haloperidol** (dopamine antagonist)

### Conduct of Anaesthesia

- Continue PD medications until start of anaesthesia
- Consider antisialagogue premedication (glycopyrronium)
- RSI and intubation due to dysphagia/GORD risk
- Regional anaesthesia advantages for airway/respiratory/cardiovascular cases
- Hypotension common and difficult to treat
- Domperidone (doesn't cross BBB) may be used safely

### Postoperative Care

- **Primary consideration:** Restoration of dopaminergic therapy
- Avoid withdrawal syndromes (Parkinsonism–hyperpyrexia syndrome)
- Consider ICU for advanced disease
- For prolonged GI dysfunction: rotigotine patches or apomorphine infusions

## Cerebrovascular Events 腦血管事件

CVEs are a leading cause of death and disability. About 85% are ischaemic, 15% haemorrhagic.

### When to Operate After CVE

- **Delay surgery for 9 months** if possible after CVE
- Risk decreases with time but remains elevated
- **TIAs:** Require urgent investigation; delay non-emergency surgery
- **Emergency surgery:** Should not be delayed; surgery within 3 days may have better outcome

### Preoperative Assessment

- Older age, frailty, hypertension, diabetes, respiratory/cardiac disease
- Neurological examination baseline
- ECG for AF
- Assess bulbar involvement
- Continue antiepileptic drugs

### Antiplatelet/Anticoagulant Therapy

- Continue antiplatelet/anticoagulant if possible
- Balance CVE risk vs surgical bleeding risk
- Discuss with specialist if on anticoagulation

### Conduct of Anaesthesia

- Continue antihypertensive medication
- Maintain cerebral perfusion pressure
- Avoid hypotension (can cause watershed infarct)

## Epilepsy 癲癇

### Preoperative Assessment

- Type and frequency of seizures
- Current antiepileptic drugs (AEDs)
- Last seizure
- Seizure control adequacy
- Triggers

### Antiepileptic Drugs

| Drug | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Phenytoin | CYP450 inducer; many interactions |
| Carbamazepine | CYP450 inducer |
| Valproate | Hepatotoxic; platelets affected |
| Levetiracetam | Few interactions |
| Lamotrigine | Many interactions |

### Perioperative Management

- Continue AEDs perioperatively
- Give as usual with sip of water
- Consider IV formulation if NBM
- Avoid drugs lowering seizure threshold
- PONV prophylaxis important

### Status Epilepticus

- Defined as >5 min continuous seizure or repeated seizures without recovery
- **Treatment:** Benzodiazepine (midazolam, diazepam, lorazepam) → Phenytoin/fosphenytoin → Levetiracetam/valproate → Consider GA/intubation

## Anaesthesia in Spinal Cord Lesions 脊髓損傷的手術麻醉

### Acute Phase (<6 months post-injury)

- **Hyperkalaemia risk** with suxamethonium (up to 6 months)
- Autonomic dysreflexia risk (T6 and above)
- Temperature regulation impaired

### Chronic Phase (>6 months)

- Suxamethonium normally safe
- **Autonomic dysreflexia:** Sudden hypertension, bradycardia, sweating, flushing
  - Triggered by bladder distension, bowel impaction, skin stimulation
  - Treatment: Remove trigger, GTN/hydralazine

### Anaesthetic Considerations

- Risk of pressure sores (immobility)
- DVT prophylaxis essential
- Temperature monitoring
- RSI if cervical injury
- Bulky leg bags for urinary catheter

## Neuromuscular Disorders 神經肌肉疾病

### Myasthenia Gravis 重症肌無力

**Autoimmune disorder with antibodies against acetylcholine receptors**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Weakness | Fatigable; worsens with activity |
| Ocular | Ptosis, diplopia common (90%) |
| Respiratory | Risk of myasthenic crisis |
| Bulbar | Dysphagia, aspiration risk |

**Perioperative:**

- Continue pyridostigmine
- Steroid cover if on chronic steroids
- Risk of cholinergic crisis (too much AChE inhibitor)
- Avoid drugs that worsen NMJ (aminoglycosides, quinolones, magnesium)
- Consider plasma exchange pre-op for severe cases

### Guillain-Barré Syndrome 格林-巴利症候群

**Acute inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Weakness | Ascending, areflexic |
| Autonomic | BP instability, arrhythmias |
| Respiratory | May need ventilation |
| Pain | Common |

**Perioperative:**

- May need plasma exchange or IVIg
- Autonomic dysfunction: BP lability, arrhythmias
- Respiratory monitoring critical
- Avoid suxamethonium (hyperkalaemia risk)

### Motor Neurone Disease 運動神經元疾病

**Progressive neurodegenerative disorder**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Weakness | Mixed UMN/UMN signs |
| Respiratory | Risk of respiratory failure |
| Bulbar | Dysphagia, aspiration risk |

**Perioperative:**

- No specific treatments
- Respiratory function assessment essential
- Consider NIV/biPAP support
- Bulbar symptoms increase aspiration risk

### Muscular Dystrophies 肌肉營養不良

**Group of inherited muscle diseases**

| Type | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Duchenne | X-linked; cardiac involvement |
| Becker | milder form of Duchenne |
| Facioscapulohumeral | Face/shoulder weakness |

**Perioperative:**

- Cardiac evaluation (all types)
- Risk of cardiomyopathy
- Avoid suxamethonium (hyperkalaemia)
- Respiratory assessment
- Malignant hyperthermia-like reactions possible

## Multiple Sclerosis 多發性硬化症

**Autoimmune demyelinating CNS disease**

### Features

- Relapsing-remitting course
- Sensory symptoms common
- Optic neuritis
- Motor weakness
- Fatigue

### Perioperative

- Continue disease-modifying therapies
- Steroid cover if on chronic steroids
- Avoid hypothermia (worsens symptoms)
- Infections can trigger relapse
- Autonomic dysfunction possible

## Rare Conditions 罕見疾病

### Lambert-Eaton Myasthenic Syndrome

- Antibodies against presynaptic voltage-gated calcium channels
- Weakness improves with activity
- Associated with small cell lung cancer
- Similar precautions as MG

### Spinal Muscular Atrophy

- Progressive weakness
- Respiratory involvement
- Scoliosis common
- Similar precautions as muscular dystrophies

### hereditary Spastic Paraplegia

- Progressive spastic weakness
- Usually no respiratory involvement
- Standard anaesthetic precautions
