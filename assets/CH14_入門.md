## Anaesthetic Gases 麻醉氣體

### Piped Medical Gases and Scavenging System (PMGSS)

The PMGSS is part of the hospital infrastructure and supplies clinical areas with compressed O₂ and vacuum. Operating theatres are also supplied with N₂O, medical air and gas scavenging.

**Key points:**

- PMGSS is a system of seamless copper alloy pipes
- Colour-coding is used on the outside of pipes at set intervals
- Terminal outlets are labelled and colour-coded with self-sealing Schrader valves
- Each gas has a specific outlet shape and diameter to prevent misconnection
- Alarms activated at low pressure (<370 kPa) and high pressures (>500 kPa)

### Oxygen 氧氣

O₂ is stored in bulk for hospital supply, either in a Vacuum-Insulated Evaporator (VIE) or in a cylinder manifold.

**Vacuum-Insulated Evaporator (VIE):**

- Large tank of liquid O₂
- One litre of liquid O₂ can provide >800 L of gaseous O₂
- Stored at –183°C at a pressure of 1050 kPa
- Supply pressure of 400 kPa to PMGSS

### Nitrous Oxide (N₂O) 笑氣

- Stored as a liquid in the cylinder at 4500 kPa
- Gauge pressure is NOT proportional to quantity in cylinder
- High ambient temperatures could cause explosion if cylinder is full

### Medical Air 醫用空氣

- Atmospheric air dehydrated and ultrafiltered
- Supplied at two pressures: 700 kPa (surgical tools) and 400 kPa (medical/anaesthetic uses)

### Vacuum System 真空系統

**Medical vacuum:** Provides high vacuum to suction for sputum or blood. A pressure of –40 kPa or below is provided with a sustained flow of at least 40 L/min.

### Anaesthetic Gas Scavenging System

- Removes expelled anaesthetic gases to minimise staff exposure
- Provides high flow (80–130 L/min) just below atmospheric pressure
- Flow indicator shows scavenging system is working

## Anaesthetic Machine 麻醉機

The continuous-flow anaesthetic machine receives a supply of high-pressure gases, reduces the pressure to safe levels and provides accurate and controlled flow of gases and vapour to the patient.

### High-Pressure System

- Takes high-pressure gas from cylinders or wall outlets
- Steps down to working pressure at flow-control valves
- Safety features include antihypoxia and low-pressure alarms
- If O₂ supply fails, N₂O supply is also stopped

### Low-Pressure System

- Extends from flow-control valves/flowmeters to common gas outlet
- In modern machines: gases set digitally with electronic control
- Traditional machines use flowmeters with rotating bobbin in fluted glass column

### Vaporisers

- Provide precise concentrations of volatile agents
- Traditional: variable-bypass plenum vaporisers (calibrated mechanical devices)
- Newer: electronically controlled devices

### Common Gas Outlet

- Standardized: 22 mm external and 15 mm internal connector
- Final pathway of gases and volatile agents

### Oxygen Flush

- Rapidly provides 100% O₂ to common gas outlet at high flow (30–75 L/min)
- Bypasses low-pressure system; no anaesthetic gases
- Potential for barotrauma

## Breathing Systems 呼吸系統

### Circle Breathing Circuit 循環式呼吸系統

The circle breathing circuit is versatile and efficient. It comprises:

- Inspiratory limb
- Expiratory limb
- Reservoir bag
- Unidirectional valves
- CO₂ absorber

**Features:**

- Low-resistance corrugated tubing (22 mm external, 15 mm internal)
- Unidirectional ('flutter') valves prevent backflow
- APL valve allows adjustment of system pressure
- Reservoir bag allows hand ventilation and visualisation of spontaneous breathing
- CO₂ absorber contains calcium hydroxide pellets

### Other Adjuncts

- O₂ analyser always positioned on inspiratory limb
- Heat and moisture exchange (HME) filter provides bacterial/viral barrier
- HME minimises loss of heat and moisture (up to 70% relative humidity)

## Ventilation 通氣

### Controlled Ventilation 控制通氣

**Indications:**

- Deep planes of anaesthesia
- Muscle paralysis
- Thoracic surgery
- Laparoscopic surgery
- Prolonged procedures

### Intermittent Positive Pressure Ventilation (IPPV)

**Parameters:**

- Tidal volume: 6–8 mL/kg ideal body weight
- Respiratory rate: 10–14 breaths/min
- I:E ratio: 1:2 (inspiratory:expiratory)
- PEEP: 3–5 cmH₂O (optional)

### Lung Protective Ventilation 肺保護性通氣

- Low tidal volume (6–8 mL/kg)
- Limiting plateau pressure (<30 cmH₂O)
- Adequate PEEP
- Recruitment manoeuvres

### Pressure Control Ventilation 壓力控制通氣

- Set inspiratory pressure
- Variable tidal volume based on lung compliance
- Useful for thoracic surgery, patients with bronchopleural fistula

### Synchronized Intermittent Mandatory Ventilation (SIMV)

- Guaranteed minimum respiratory rate
- Patient can trigger additional breaths
- Used for weaning from mechanical ventilation

### Positive End-Expiratory Pressure (PEEP)

- Maintains alveolar recruitment at end-expiration
- Prevents atelectasis
- Typical values: 3–10 cmH₂O

## Airway Equipment 氣道設備

### Face Masks 面罩

- Various sizes for adults and children
- Proper seal essential for effective ventilation
- Cuffed vs uncuffed (children)

### Supraglottic Airway Devices (SADs) 聲门上氣道

**Examples:**

- Classic LMA
- ProSeal LMA (double lumen)
- i-gel
- LMA Supreme

**Indications:**

- Spontaneous ventilation
- Short procedures
- Difficult airway (rescue)

### Endotracheal Tubes (ETT) 氣管內管

**Types:**

- Oral/nasal
- Cuffed/uncuffed
- Reinforced
- Double-lumen

**Sizes:**

- Adult male: 7.0–8.5 mm
- Adult female: 6.0–7.5 mm
- Children: Formula: age/4 + 4

### Intubation Aids 插管輔助工具

**Direct Laryngoscopy:**

- Macintosh blade (sizes 2, 3, 4)
- McCoy blade

**Videolaryngoscopes:**

- GlideScope
- C-MAC
- Airtraq

**Other Aids:**

- Gum elastic bougie
- Fibreoptic scope
- Light wand

### Difficult Airway Equipment 困難氣道設備

**Always have available:**

- Various blade sizes
- Videolaryngoscope
- Bougie
- Fibreoptic scope
- SADs
- Cricothyrotomy kit

## Long-Term Venous Access 長期靜脈通路

### Peripheral Venous Cannulae 周邊靜脈導管

**Sizes:**

- 14G (large bore) - rapid transfusion
- 16G - major surgery
- 18G - general use
- 20G - elective surgery
- 22G - children, fragile veins

### Central Venous Catheters (CVC) 中央靜脈導管

**Sites:**

- Internal jugular
- Subclavian
- Femoral

**Complications:**

- Pneumothorax
- Arterial puncture
- Infection
- Thrombosis

### Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICC) 周邊置入中央導管

- Long-term IV access
- Inserted via peripheral vein (basilic, cephalic)
- Tip positioned in superior vena cava

### Hickman Line 希克曼導管

- Central venous catheter with subcutaneous port
- For long-term chemotherapy, TPN
- Reduced infection risk compared to external catheters

## Checking Anaesthetic Equipment 麻醉設備檢查

**Pre-use checks (AAGBI 2012):**

1. **Self-check** of machine if computerised
2. **Gas supplies:** Cylinders, pipelines, connections
3. **Ventilation:** Manual ventilation, ventilator settings
4. **Breathing system:** Integrity, APL valve, CO₂ absorber
5. **Airway equipment:** Laryngoscope, ETT, SADs
6. **Monitoring:** Alarms set, appropriate limits
7. **Suction:** Functional
8. **Rescue equipment:** Difficult airway trolley
