## Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA) 類風濕性關節炎

RA is a chronic, systemic inflammatory disorder mainly involving synovial joints, but with extra-articular effects. Overall prevalence in the UK is 1.5 per 100,000 in males, and 3.6 per 100,000 in females.

### Preoperative Assessment

#### Articular

- **Temporomandibular:** Assess for limited mouth opening.
- **Cricoarytenoid:** Fixation of the cricoarytenoid joints may lead to voice changes or stridor. Minimal oedema may lead to airway obstruction postoperatively.
- **Atlantoaxial subluxation:** Occurs in up to 25% of severe rheumatoid patients. Risk factors include disease duration >8 years, glucocorticoid use, seropositivity and nodular/erosive peripheral joint disease.

| Table 10.1: Atlantoaxial Subluxation |
|---|
| **Type** | **Incidence** | **Pathology** | **Imaging** |
| Anterior | 80% | Destruction of transverse ligament | >3mm between odontoid and arch of C1 on lateral flexion |
| Posterior | 5% | Destruction of odontoid peg | Loss of odontoid peg on lateral extension |
| Vertical | 10–20% | Odontoid moves upward through foramen magnum | >4.5mm of odontoid above McGregor line |
| Lateral | Uncommon | Destruction of C1/C2 facet joints | >2mm difference in lateral alignment |

#### Non-Articular

- **CVS:** Increased risk of CAD. Systemic vasculitis may lead to arterial occlusion. Pericarditis and pericardial effusions uncommon. At risk of VTE.
- **Respiratory:** Interstitial lung disease, pulmonary nodules, pleural disease, obliterative bronchiolitis.
- **Haematological:** Normocytic anaemia of chronic disease. Felty's syndrome (splenomegaly and neutropenia).
- **Nervous system:** Central (axial involvement) and peripheral compression neuropathies.
- **Sjögren's syndrome:** Dry eyes put patients at risk of corneal ulceration.

### Drugs in the Perioperative Period

- **Steroids:** Minimise prior to surgery. Steroid supplementation if indicated.
- **NSAIDs:** Continue to enable early mobilisation.
- **DMARDs:** Continue as little evidence that omission reduces complications. If leucopenic or for high-risk procedures, consult rheumatologist.
- **Biological DMARDs:** Consider ceasing at least one dosing interval prior to surgery. Rituximab should be stopped 3–6 months prior, tocilizumab 4 weeks prior.

### Operative Considerations

- Consider regional anaesthesia if possible to minimise airway instrumentation.
- Unless certain that cervical spine is stable, treat all rheumatoid patients as if they have an unstable spine.
- Restriction of cervical spine motion (ROCSM) is necessary during airway manipulation.
- Consider videolaryngoscopy or fibreoptic intubation if difficulties are anticipated.
- Ensure careful positioning and padding of vulnerable areas.

## Ankylosing Spondylitis 僵直性脊椎炎

Inflammatory arthritis of the sacroiliac joints and spine, leading to ankylosis and 'bamboo spine'. Associated with HLA-B27 in >90% of cases. More common in males, with peak age onset in the 3rd decade.

### Articular

- Progressive kyphosis and fixation of the spine may hinder intubation.
- Atlantoaxial subluxation and myelopathy can occur rarely.
- Limited mouth opening from temporomandibular involvement.
- Fibreoptic intubation usually preferred.
- At risk of occult cervical fracture with minimal trauma.
- Cricoarytenoid arthritis may make cords susceptible to trauma and oedema.
- Spinal anaesthesia often difficult; consider paramedian approach.
- Restrictive pulmonary disease from diminished chest expansion.

### Non-Articular

- Apical fibrosis
- Aortic regurgitation (1%)
- Amyloid may cause renal involvement
- Cauda equina syndrome may occur

## Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE) 系統性紅斑狼瘡

Chronic multisystem autoimmune disease, commonest in young females. Characterised by presence of numerous antibodies including antinuclear antibody.

### Preoperative Assessment

- **Skin and joint involvement** is common, as are oral and pharyngeal ulcerations.
- **CVS:** Pericarditis in 15% of cases. Myocarditis, endocarditis and conduction abnormalities. Raynaud's phenomenon 30%. CAD.
- **Respiratory:** Infections and PE common. Pleuritis and pleural effusion.
- **Neurological:** Cranial and peripheral nerve lesions. Depression, psychosis and seizures possible.
- **Renal:** Glomerulonephritis, which may lead to nephrotic syndrome and renal failure.
- **Haematological:** Cytopenias, clotting disorders or hypercoagulable states. Antiphospholipid syndrome may be present.

### Anaesthesia

- May be absolute or relative contraindications to neuraxial blocks in patients on anticoagulants or with coagulopathy.
- Maintenance of normothermia.
- Laryngeal erythema and oedema are common—minimise trauma to the airway.
- Consider hourly urine output and invasive monitoring.
- Steroid supplementation.
- Strict asepsis with invasive procedures.
- Perioperative VTE prophylaxis.

## Systemic Sclerosis (Scleroderma) 全身性硬化症

Progressive multisystem disease characterised by widespread vascular dysfunction and fibrosis.

### Types

- **Limited cutaneous form (CREST):** Calcinosis, Raynaud's, oesophageal dysfunction, sclerodactyly, telangiectasia (60% of cases)
- **Diffuse cutaneous form:** More widespread effects and high mortality

### Relevant Considerations

- **CVS:** Raynaud's, pericarditis or myocardial fibrosis. Pulmonary hypertension common.
- **Pulmonary:** Fibrosing alveolitis (40% in diffuse form).
- **Renal:** May develop renal crisis associated with malignant hypertension.
- **GI:** Oesophageal reflux invariable.
- **Airway:** Limited mouth opening and tightened skin around face/neck. Difficult mask ventilation and intubation.
- **Dermal:** Thickening and contractures—difficulty with IV access and positioning.

## Scoliosis 脊椎側彎

Progressive lateral curvature of the spine with added rotation. May lead to restrictive ventilatory defect, hypoxia, hypercapnia and pulmonary hypertension.

### Causes

- Idiopathic (75%)
- Secondary to: Muscular dystrophies, poliomyelitis, cerebral palsy, Friedreich's ataxia, Marfan syndrome, Ehlers-Danlos syndrome

### Conduct of Anaesthesia

- Formal PFTs. Cobb angle exceeding 65° is likely associated with compromised respiratory function.
- Check for pulmonary hypertension and right heart failure.
- Some muscular dystrophies may be associated with cardiac abnormalities.
- May be difficult laryngoscopy.
- Neuraxial anaesthesia more difficult.
- Intraoperative spinal cord monitoring recommended for spinal deformity corrective surgery.
- Invasive arterial monitoring and potential significant blood loss.
- Multimodal analgesia including regional techniques.

## Achondroplasia 軟骨發育不全

The commonest form of dwarfism caused by premature ossification of bones.

### Considerations

- **Airway:** Large head and tongue, mid-facial hypoplasia, obesity and small larynx may lead to difficulties. Foramen magnum stenosis is common.
- **Avoid hyperextension during intubation.**
- Central and peripheral venous access often difficult.
- OSA is common.
- Restrictive ventilatory defects may occur.
- Regional techniques may be difficult.
- Epidural space is often narrowed with spinal canal stenosis. Volume of LA needed is reduced.
- The patient is of normal intelligence.
